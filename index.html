Quality (QA) Career Framework

Dual track competency mapping and progression for the Editing Quality team (Creative Operations) DRAFT

Summary

This framework replaces the single-band QA structure with a clear seven-level ladder: three individual-contributor levels (P1–P3) that build craft and quality authority, then four management levels (M1–M4). Senior specialists (P3) grow by being promoted into management at M1. Promotion follows the company rule: master your level, perform the next, and reach into the one above.

Career-track map

P1–P2 reviewers  ·  P3 senior specialist  ·  M1–M4 management.   Horizontal position reflects the autonomy tier (L1–L5). P3 → M1 is a promotion into management (L3 → L4).

One ladder, no fork. Senior individual contributors (P3) grow by moving into management at M1. The dashed segment marks that promotion step from individual contributor into people leadership.

P-Track — Individual Contributor

The craft ladder: P1 → P2 → P3. Reviewers (P1–P2) apply the quality standards and run the recurring work; the Senior Quality Specialist (P3) owns and shapes the standards, adjudicates the hard calls, and deputizes for the Team Lead. P3 is the top individual-contributor level — a senior specialist grows by being promoted into management at M1.

Glossary — Reviewer vs. Specialist

The ladder changes character at the P2→P3 seam. Hover the dotted terms in the table to see them again.

Reviewer (P1–P2)

Applies the quality standards and runs the recurring work — reviews against the rubric, investigates, executes routine calibration sessions, and escalates anything non-routine. Operates the system; does not change it. Placeholder for your formal one-line definition.

Specialist (P3)

Owns and shapes the standards — builds the rubric, defines the vendor bar, adjudicates exceptions, gives final Delivery QA sign-off, and deputizes for M1 on shift. Improves the system, not just individual outputs. Placeholder for your formal one-line definition.

Competencies are cumulative. Each level includes everything below it, plus what\'s shown in its column. The guiding seam: P2 runs the recurring cadence; P3 owns the standard, the exceptions, and the senior sign-offs.

Why can’t M1 just do P3’s job? They are different jobs a level apart. P3 defines the standard — owns the rubric, the vendor bar and the AI-QC methodology, gives final Delivery QA sign-off, and is the senior craft authority. M1 owns the people and delivery — leads the whole team, runs 1:1s, holds final authority on disputes (with P3’s technical support), and is accountable for the team hitting the standard. A P3 is promoted into M1; the step adds people leadership and delivery accountability on top of the craft depth.

M-Track — Management

The people-and-delivery ladder, M1 → M4. A Senior Quality Specialist (P3) is promoted into M1. Managers keep the full P1–P3 craft baseline — including AI quality-checking — and add people leadership, delivery ownership, and final authority on top. The steps up are strategic depth and scope, not just headcount: M1 leads the team, M2 owns how the team operates, M3 also heads AI/Magic Department QA, M4 owns function strategy.

Competencies are cumulative. Each level includes everything below it (and the full P1–P3 craft foundation), plus what\'s shown in its column.

Budget justifications

GLSS Lean Six Sigma — White (P2) / Yellow (M1) / Green (M2)

White Belt is free and sits at P2 (process-improvement vocabulary). The paid asks begin at management: Yellow Belt at M1 (~$120–150/seat) teaches DMAIC, process mapping and root-cause through project work — the basis for a Team Lead’s systems thinking; Green Belt at M2 (~$300/seat) lets a senior lead run improvement projects. Team subscriptions lower per-head cost; fund by level as people are promoted, not all at once.

Coursera — Feedback & Coaching for Everyone (ASU) — M-track

~US$59/mo or ~US$399/yr per learner (Coursera Plus covers this plus the feedback course). The free course covers basics; managers need the full specialization — coaching psychology, managing up/down, accountability. Cost control: apply for Coursera financial aid (free) first; budget only for denials.

AI quality-checking is a client-driven priority, not a nice-to-have. Clients have escalated AI hallucinations they detected in delivered work — specifically in the Photoshop-heavy advanced edits (background removal, hair strays, object removal) where generative AI fabricates detail. This makes the P1 hallucination-spotting baseline non-negotiable and justifies the internal AI-QC training. The Anthropic Academy "AI Capabilities & Limitations" certificate (free) sits underneath it; the photo-specific failure modes are taught internally from real escalation examples.

Optional / deferred

Adobe Certified Professional (Lightroom/Photoshop) and the Google Data Analytics Certificate are optional recognized upgrades — fund per-person only where a portable credential is wanted. EF SET proctoring is likely $0 in year one.

Bottom line: the framework is deliverable at near-zero cost. Realistic paid ask = one GLSS team subscription + a pool of Coursera Plus seats for confirmed M-track candidates.

Proposed internal course

AI Frameworks for Creative Quality-Checking

No credible external course teaches this — the failure modes are specific to our pipeline. Built and owned in-house, it is the direct answer to the client escalations. P3 develops the methodology; M1 owns the final call on adopting it.

Why internal
The AI errors clients flagged are specific to our editing pipeline (Lightroom + Photoshop retouch). A generic AI course can't teach what our clients caught; our SMEs can.
Foundation
External, free-cert: Anthropic Academy — AI Capabilities & Limitations — teaches why generative AI fabricates detail.
Application
Internal module on our specific failure modes: a labeled gallery of real (anonymized) escalation examples — good vs. hallucinated — with a checklist per edit type (background removal · hair strays · object removal) and a calibration quiz.
Owner
P3 (Senior Quality Specialist) develops and maintains the content; M1 owns adoption as the team's AI-QC standard. It doubles as calibration material. Developing it counts toward a P3's progression into M1.
Certification
Internal completion record (proof of completion), refreshed as new failure modes appear.
Who completes it
P1+ (the hallucination-spotting baseline) and M1+ (so managers can hold their teams to it).
Cost to fund
SME time to build and maintain — no licensing fee. The only "spend" is the hours, which sit inside existing P3 and M1 responsibilities.

Rollout

  1. Baseline. Run EF SET on all current QAs and map each person against the matrices. (Free, ~1 week.)
  2. Slot. Place each QA at P1–P3 by demonstrated scope; identify P3s ready to be developed toward the M1 promotion.
  3. Gap plan. For each person, assign the free-cert courses for their target level plus any English gap from step 1.
  4. Budget ask. Take the L&D tab to leadership for the GLSS team subscription + Coursera Plus pool.
  5. Cadence. Reassess against the matrices and autonomy signals each cycle; promote only when Master + Perform + Stretch and the right autonomy rung are consistently met. Instrument the quantitative autonomy & systems-thinking signals over the next 1–2 quarters.
